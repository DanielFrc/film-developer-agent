Here is a detailed black and white film development recipe for Rollei Retro 400S (120) with Rodinal:

**1. Materials:**

* Rollei Retro 400S (120) film
* Rodinal developer (500ml)
* Dilution water (50ml)
* Stop bath (250ml)
* Fixer (500ml)
* Water (for washing and final rinse)
* Thermometer
* Film reels or spools
* Developer tray or container with a lid

**2. Prerequisites:**

* Ensure the development room is well-ventilated and at 20°C (68°F).
* Wear protective gloves, safety glasses, and a lab coat.
* Familiarize yourself with the Ilford method of washing.

**3. Developer Preparation (500ml):**

1. Weigh out 250g of Rodinal powder.
2. Gradually add 450ml of water to the powder, stirring until fully dissolved.
3. Add 50ml of dilution water to create a 1+50 solution.

**4. Pre-soak:** No pre-soaking is required for this recipe.

**5. Developer Agitation:**

* Initial agitation: Gently agitate the developer for 10 seconds.
* Subsequent agitation: Every 2 minutes, agitate the developer for 5 seconds (a total of 6 agitations).

**6. Stop Bath:** Use a 1+4 Acetic Acid solution for 3 minutes.

**7. Fixer:** 

* Normal fix: 8 minutes
* Rapid fix: 3 minutes

**8. Washing (Ilford method):**

1. Fill the washing container with water at 20°C (68°F).
2. Invert and agitate the film for 5 minutes.
3. Drain the water, fill again, and invert/agitate for 10 minutes.
4. Repeat step 3 one more time.

**9. Drying:**

1. Use a drying rack or a clean towel to gently blot excess moisture from the film.
2. Store the dried film in its original packaging or a cool, dry place to maintain humidity.

**10. General Precautions & Advice:**

* Always handle chemicals with gloves and in a well-ventilated area.
* Ensure the development room is at 20°C (68°F) for optimal results.
* Follow the recommended times and dilutions for this recipe.
* Dispose of chemicals according to local regulations.
* Keep children, pets, and ignition sources away from the development process.

Remember to prioritize lab safety throughout the development process.
